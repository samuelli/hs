Started by the students of CSEsoc, the Computing Club is a chance for High School students to further explore computing and get a head start in the world of technology. The Computing club is a school without the formality and also a great chance to meet other students with the same passion, all while learning a great skill.

At the Computing Club, we offer a variety of modules that cater to all skill levels, no one gets left out. As of now, all our modules are free.

Intro to Programming

Introduction to Programming is designed for students that are interested in computer science but have not yet started programming. In the course, students will learn to program in Python and start to create apps that will teach them the basics of computer coding.

Details

Difficulty: Beginner

Programming Languages: Python

When: Mondays from 4-6.30pm, starting on 13th August

Length: 9 weeks

Where: Banjo lab, K17, UNSW campus

What to bring: Students are not required to bring anything for the course but may want to have a notebook, pen or laptop for note taking and possibly an afternoon snack.

Cost: Free!

Adventures in Recreational Computer Science

ARCS is designed to show students that there is more to computer science than just learning how to program. Each week will feature a different topic, and each week will stand independent of the rest - so if you have to miss a week, don't worry! Exactly what we will cover will depend on what the students want to know, though our initial range of topics includes: How to win programming competitions, Computer security, Computer graphics, Artificial Intelligence, and Maze generation.

Details

Difficulty: Intermediate or expert

Programming Languages: All

When: Mondays from 4-7pm, starting on 13th August

Length: 9 weeks

Where: Seminar Room, K17, UNSW campus

What to bring: Students are not required to bring anything for the course but laptops are preferable

Cost: Free!

About

The High School Computing Club aims to bring together students with similar interests, where the CSE students are there to provide guidance and high school students support each other in developing their skills and expanding their knowledge.

The motivation for running High School Computing Club came when a bunch of CSE students tutored at CSE's winter school. They noticed that a lot of high school students are enthusiastic about computing, but there was little personal support available when they try to learn on their own.
