The UNSW Computing Club is an initiative started by the students of UNSW's CSE School to help and educate high school students with an interest in computing.

Through the support of the club, high school students will be able to learn the basic skills of computing as well as broaden their understanding of the discipline.

Computing skills not only allow you to develop programs but they also build strong methodical and creative thinking, attributes that are valued not only in computing but also in many other aspects of their schooling and future careers.

Intro to Programming

Our first course is aimed at introducing students with no prior knowledge of programming to good coding principles. Through this course, students will gain knowledge on the basics of code writing and how to approach problems and solve them with computers. Our approach is to present all this information through practical exercises, such as building small games and applications.

More specifically, at the end of this course students will feel confident with: variables, if-else conditionals, loops, functions, arithmetic in code, true-false statements, good coding style, using libraries/modules/documentation, classes and the programming language Python.

We emphasize that this course is for students that have not previously programmed, and is paced so that these students have time to experiment with what they have learnt while also having time to ask questions.

Details

When: Weekly, 4pm until 6pm, on a day that has yet to be confirmed. Starting the week 6th of August

Where: Located in UNSW COMPUTING labs, K17

What to bring: Students are not required to bring anything for the course but may want to have a notebook, pen or laptop for note taking and possibly an afternoon snack.

Cost: Free!
